Filipino Soups

1. Pork Sinigang  


Sinigang is a pork-based Filipino soup. Serve with rice and extra sauce (soy or fish sauce).This soup have a sour and strong umami that will keep you in your seat. The sourness of the soup brings a wonderful flavor that will keep you in sipping its soup. In addition to its sourness the tender meat also brings a wonderful taste that is perfect match to the soup.


Recipe by Robyn Michelle

By Daniel Rojas  |   November 16, 2022

Photo by Knorr


PrepTime  CookTime

 10 mins    1 hour

Total Time:  Servings

1 hrs 10 mins    6


Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 (1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger, chopped
  • 2 plum tomatoes, cut into 1/2-inch dice
  • 1 pound bone-in pork chops
  • 4 cups water, more if needed
  • 1 (1.41 ounce) package tamarind soup base (such as Knorr®)
  • ½ pound fresh green beans, trimmed


Procedures

  1. Step1
    Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. Season with salt.
  2. Step2
    Stir in ginger, tomatoes, and pork chops. Cover and reduce heat to medium-low. Turn the pork occasionally, until browned.
  3. Step3
    Pour in water and tamarind soup base. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the pork is tender and cooked through, about 30 minutes.
  4. Step4
    Stir in green beans and cook until tender.




2. Miswa and Patola Soup  


Miswa at Patola Soup is a delicious Filipino dish made of sponge gourd, ground pork, and Chinese vermicelli. It’s hearty and tasty on its own or as a side dish.


By Daniel Rojas  |   November 16, 2022

Photo by Yummy Kitchen


PrepTime  CookTime

 5 mins    30 mins

Total Time:  Servings

 35 mins    3


Ingredients

  • 3 medium patola
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2-pound ground pork
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 ounces miswa noodles
  • salt and pepper to taste


Procedures

  1. Step1
    Cut off both ends of the patola and discard. Using a vegetable peeler, peel the skin. Slice the peeled gourd crosswise into about 1/4-inch thick.
  2. Step2
    In a wide pan over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic and cook until softened
  3. Step3
    Add pork and cook, breaking apart with the back of a spoon, until lightly browned.
  4. Step4
    Add fish sauce and continue to cook, stirring occasionally, for about 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Step5
    Add water and bring to a boil, skimming any scum that may float on top.
  6. Step6
    Lower heat and cook for about 10 minutes or until pork is tender.
  7. Step7
    Add patola and cook for about 3 to 5 minutes or until tender yet crisp.
  8. Step8
    Add miswa noodles and cook for about 1 to 2 minutes or until softened.
  9. Step9
    Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.




3. Chicken Sotanghon Soup  


The Filipino version of chicken noodle soup is Chicken Sotanghon Soup. Shredded chicken and sotanghon noodles are used in this soup. Carrots and cabbage are also included. The addition of roasted garlic, scallions, and fish sauce seasoning makes it stand out.


By Daniel Rojas  |   November 16, 2022

Photo by Eat Like Pinoy


PrepTime  CookTime

 20mins    18 mins

Total Time:  Servings

 38 mins    4


Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 thumb-size ginger, peeled and julienned
  • 1 pound chicken wings, tips removed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup Chinese celery (kinchay), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 7 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon atchuete powder
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and julienned
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 ounces (about 2 coils) sotanghon
  • 1/2 head napa cabbage, shredded
  • 3 hardboiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • fried garlic bits
  • green onions, chopped


Procedures

  1. Step1
    In a large pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions, garlic, and ginger and cook until softened.
  2. Step2
    Add chicken and cook, stirring regularly, until lightly browned and juices run clear.
  3. Step3
    Add celery and cook, stirring regularly, for about 1 to 2 minutes or until softened.
  4. Step4
    Add fish sauce and cook for about 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Step5
    Add chicken broth and bring to a boil, skimming any scum that floats on top. Lower heat, cover, and continue to cook until chicken is cooked through.
  6. Step6
    In a bowl, combine about 1/2 cup of the hot broth and atsuete powder. Stir until atsuete is dissolved. Add atsuete water to pot.
  7. Step7
    Add carrots and cook for about 1 to 2 minutes or until half-done. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Step8
    Add sotanghon noodles and push down into broth to soften, stirring gently to separate.
  9. Step9
    Ladle soup into bowls and top with boiled eggs, fried garlic bits and green onions. Serve hot.
  10. Step10
    When noodles have slightly softened, add cabbage and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes or until noodles are cooked and vegetables are tender yet crisp
