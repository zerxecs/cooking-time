Filipino Meal dishes

1. Adobo  


A family favourite in the Philippines, this soya-sauce-based dish is the perfect complement to freshly steamed rice.


By Daniel Rojas  |   November 16, 2022

Photo By Emma Fishman


PrepTime  CookTime

 20mins    45 mins

Total Time:  Servings

1 hrs 5 mins    6


Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf


Procedures

  1. Step1
    Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  2. Step2
    Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  3. Step3
    Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.




2. Pancit Bihon 


Pancit Bihon is a Filipino noodle dish that is second only to rice in popularity. This was brought by the Chinese and has since been localized. This recipe for Pancit Bihon combines "Bihon," or rice sticks, with pork, chicken, and vegetables. During Fiesta, birthday parties, and other special occasions, this dish is always served.


By Daniel Rojas  |   November 16, 2022

Photo by Rezel Kealoha


PrepTime  CookTime

 15mins    40 mins

Total Time:  Servings

 55 mins    8


Ingredients

  • 150g (5 oz) pork belly, skin remove and thinly sliced
  • 200g (7 oz) chicken thigh, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup shredded carrot
  • ½ cup finely sliced Chinese cabbage (also called wombok or Napa cabbage)
  • 50g (1.7 oz) snow peas
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 180g (6 oz) dried vermicelli rice noodles (bihon noodles)
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 spring onions (scallions), sliced
  • lime wedges to serve


Procedures

  1. Step1Place the pork belly and chicken into a cold large pan or wok. Turn the heat on high and when the pork and chicken start sizzling, allow the fat to render for 3-4 minutes or until the meat pieces are golden and cooked through. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the pork and chicken to a plate (leave the fat behind in the pan).
  2. Step2
    Add the garlic and onion to the same frying pan. Stir-fry over high heat for half a minute. Then add the carrot, cabbage, and snow peas. Stir-fry for another minute. Then add the chicken stock, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce and sugar. Stir to combine. Then place the noodles into the sauce. Use your tongs to toss the noodles as they cook in the sauce. Continue cooking and stir-frying for 2-3 minutes or until the noodles are tender and soaked up all the sauce. Add the pork and chicken back into the pan along with a pinch of pepper and the spring onions. Toss until combined. Divide among serving bowls. Serve with a lime wedge on the side.




3.Chicken Pochero  


Pochero, also known as Puchero, is a popular Filipino stew. The Chiken Pochero is a well balanced meal as it have meat and vegetables. As such if you are looking for nutritous meal have a try cooking and eating Chiken Pochero.


By Daniel Rojas  |   November 16, 2022

Photo by Pinterest


PrepTime  CookTime

 10mins    1 hour

Total Time:  Servings

1 hrs 10 mins    4


Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup canola oil
  • 3 saba bananas, peeled and halved
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1 onion, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 3 pounds chicken, cut into serving parts
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 chorizo de bilbao, cut into 1-inch-thick pieces
  • 1 cup garbanzo beans
  • 12 pieces green beans, ends trimmed
  • 1/2 napa cabbage, end trimmed and leaves halved
  • 1 bunch pechay, ends trimmed and leaves separated
  • salt and pepper to taste


Procedures

  1. Step1
    In a pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add bananas and fry until lightly browned. Remove from pan and drain on paper towels
  2. Step2
    Add potatoes and fry until lightly browned. Remove from pan and drain on paper towels.
  3. Step3
    Remove excess oil except for about 2 tablespoons. Add onions and garlic and cook until softened.
  4. Step4
    Add chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and juices run clear.
  5. Step5
    Add fish sauce and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. Step6
    Add tomato sauce and water and bring to a boil, skimming any scum that accumulates on top.
  7. Step7
    Lower heat, cover, and cook for about 15 to 20 minutes or until chicken is cooked.
  8. Step8
    Add potatoes and cook until tender.
  9. Step9
    Add chorizo de bilbao, garbanzo beans, and bananas. Cook until heated through.
  10. Step10
    Add green beans and napa cabbage and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes.
  11. Step11
    Add pechay and cook for about 1 minute or until vegetables are tender yet crisp. Serve hot.
  12. Step12
    Season with salt and pepper to taste.
